How Does The Love Calculator Work?

The Love Calculator employs an intriguing algorithm based on counting specific letters within two names. Here are the detailed steps:

  1. Concatenate the boy's name and the girl's name into a single string (for example, “ALICE” and ‘BOB’ concatenate to “ALICEBOB”).
  2. Count the occurrences of the letters L, O, V, E, and S in the combined string.
  3. Create a numerical sequence based on these counts (e.g., L:2, O:2, V:1, E:2, S:0 becomes “22120”).
  4. Repeatedly add adjacent digits until a two-digit number is obtained (e.g., 2+2=4, 2+1=3, 1+2=3, 2+0=2 → “4332”; then 4+3=7, 3+3=6, 3+2=5 → “765”; then 7+6=13→3, 6+5=11→1 → “31”).
  5. The final two digits represent your love percentage (in this example, 31%).

How To Do The Love Calculator On Paper?

The paper love calculator determines compatibility by counting the frequency of letters in two names that appear in combinations with “love” or “true love,” then adding adjacent numbers until a two-digit percentage is obtained. Common methods include assigning letter values (e.g., L=2, O=1, V=1, E=1) or using the “FLAMES” algorithm.
I will use two new paired names: Tom and Jerry. Here are the steps to create a paper love calculator:
Step 1: Write the Names
Write down both names: Tom and Jerry.
Step 2: Count the letters
Count the total number of times each letter appears in both names:
Tom: T, o, m
Jerry: J, e, r, r, y
Combine letters and count:
T: 1 time (in Tom)
o: 1 time (in Tom)
m: 1 time (in Tom)
J: 1 time (in Jerry)
e: 1 time (in Jerry)
r: 2 times (in Jerry)
y: 1 time (in Jerry)
List the counts in the order the letters appear in the names (Tom first, then Jerry, unique letters only): T=1, o=1, m=1, J=1, e=1, r=2, y=1
Number sequence: 1, 1, 1, 1, 1, 2, 1
Step 3: Create the number sequence
Write the numbers in a row: 1, 1, 1, 1, 1, 2, 1
Step 4: Add adjacent numbers
Add pairs of adjacent numbers (starting from the first number with the second, the second with the third, and so on):
1 + 1 = 2
1 + 1 = 2
1 + 1 = 2
1 + 1 = 2
1 + 2 = 3
2 + 1 = 3
New number sequence: 2, 2, 2, 2, 3, 3
Step 5: Simplify to a Percentage (Repeated Addition)
Repeat the addition process until only two-digit numbers remain.
Second Round of Addition: For the sequence 2, 2, 2, 2, 3, 3, add from both ends toward the middle as shown in the example, covering all digits:
First digit plus last digit: 2 + 3 = 5
Second number and second-to-last number: 2 + 3 = 5
Third number and third-to-last number: 2 + 2 = 4
Fourth number and fifth number: 2 + 3 = 5
New sequence: 5, 5, 4, 5
Third round of addition: For the sequence 5, 5, 4, 5, following the pattern from the example, add the first and last numbers, and the second and second-to-last numbers:
First and last numbers: 5 + 5 = 10
Second and second-to-last numbers: 5 + 4 = 9
New number sequence: 10, 9
Now only two numbers remain: 10 and 9.
Step 6: Interpreting the Result
The final love percentage is 109 (or 10 + 9 = 19%). Therefore, Tom and Jerry's compatibility is 19%.
Note: The percentage can be interpreted as either the two-digit combination (109) or the sum of the digits (19%). Common practice is to use the sum of the digits, such as 23% in the example. Thus, the compatibility here is 19%.
